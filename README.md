# Australia-Trade-Analytics
An interactive Tableau dashboard project that visualises Australia's import and export trends and tracks the history of key products like Coal, Petroleum, and Gas.

## How the Dashboards are Organised
The project is structured like a funnel, guiding you from big picture country trends down into the specific stories of individual products.
```

┌──────────────────────────┐      ┌──────────────────────────┐
│ 1. THE BIG PICTURE       ├─────►│  2. CATEGORY DRILLDOWNS  │
│ Shows the top 3 items    │      │ Deep dives into Food,    │
│ Australia buys and sells │      │ Metals, Cars, and Tech   │
└──────────────────────────┘      └──────────────────────────┘
│
┌─────────────▼────────────┐
│ 3. THE ENERGY HUB        │
│ Head-to-head analysis of │
│ Coal vs. Petroleum vs. Gas│
└──────────────────────────┘

```

### 1. The Big Picture (Top Trade Categories)
* **Australia's Pillars:** The data clearly shows that Australia relies heavily on exporting raw materials and fuels while importing machinery and vehicles to keep the country running.
* **The 2019 Peak:** Charts highlight a major spike in 2019 due to record high prices for resources like iron ore and coal.
* **Post-COVID Bounce:** Visualizations track how nearly all trade categories surged after 2020 as global markets reopened and energy demand bounced back.

### 2. Fascinating Product Stories Found in the Data
* **Food & Animals:** Shows a massive jump in cereal trade around 2019 because countries stockpiled essential foods during COVID-19. It also captures a sharp drop in sugar trade after 1995 due to industry deregulation.
* **Cars & Machinery:** Tracks the death of Australian car manufacturing. You can visually see a sharp drop in local production when Ford, Toyota, and Holden shut their local factories between 2016 and 2017, making Australia fully reliant on imported cars.
* **Gold as a Safe Haven:** Shows massive spikes in gold trade (1989–1992 and 2017–2020) when global investors panicked and used gold as a financial safety net.

## The Energy Hub: Coal vs. Petroleum vs. Gas
A dedicated dashboard explores the unique journeys of Australia’s three most important energy products:

* **Coal (Steady Export Growth):** Has grown consistently since 2015 and peaked around 2020, proving Australia's status as a primary energy supplier to Asian markets.
* **Petroleum (The Major Import):** Dominates what Australia buys from overseas, but experienced a massive dip between 2005 and 2014 due to shifting oil prices and early moves toward green energy.
* **Gas / LNG (The Booming Sector):** Surged incredibly fast between 2015 and 2024, growing more than **five times** from its lowest point as global industries looked for cleaner alternatives to coal.
